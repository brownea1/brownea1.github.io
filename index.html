About

My name is Ethan Brown and I am a Junior Software Engineering student with minors in Data Science, Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics at Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology. I have succeeded, and went above and beyond in many important classes during my first year at Rose-Hulman. Not only have I shown academic success, but I also am very involved on campus. To start, I am a member in the Pi Kappa Alpha social Fraternity and the Alpha Lambda Delta academic Fraternity. I am also involved in intramural sports, and am a member of the Chess Club.

I have taken Object-Oriented Programming, and Datastructures & Algorithm Analysis here, and did more than what was asked. For example, in my Object-Oriented Programming class, I opted into a more difficult final project and met all of the higher expectations for that project. In my Datastructures and Algorithm Analysis class, we were tasked with implementing an insertion and deletion method for Top-Down red black trees. My professor told us to implement it recursively, but I noticed that if it was done iteratively it would be more efficient. With my professors permission, I implemented insertion iteratively and my code ran faster than my professor's iterative insertion method.
